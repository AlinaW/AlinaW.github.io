Device Insight

Device insight was the first data visualization feature that was introduced at Duo Security in early 2016. The goal of this feature was to help our customers (IT administrators) to understand the health state of his devices at a glance. We let this feature live and breath for 1.5 years and then we returned to make it better.

Why did we do it in the first place?

75% of breaches happen because compromised user devices.

Every year, Verizon has been releasing a security report. And every year we have been told over and over again that most of the breaches happen because of compromised devices or compromised credentials. What are we calling here a "compromised device"? The devices that is running out of date software such as operating system, browser, or plugin. These devices are a threat to a security of a company. So we asked ourselves here at Duo, how can we help?

Because Duo provides two-factor authentication within a browser, we were able to determine which os, browser, or plugin you are using. This exact information is crucial to understanding if a device is safe to enter a work application.

Research (or get your shovel out)

  • Customer visits

    We visited 5 customer in the Bay area (including Facebook) to talk about how they set requirements around devices, what is their ideal state, and how can we at Duo help them to understand information around devices.

  • Tech support tickets

    Duo's customer success team has been leveraging Salesforce to keep track of all tech support calls and requests.

  • Data and analytics

    Using internal logging system (Kibana) and Google analytics, I was able to dig into how often customers interact with the current Device page and if they perform any actions on the page.

  • UX interviews
